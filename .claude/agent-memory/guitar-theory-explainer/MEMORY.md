# Guitar Theory Explainer - Teaching Memory

## Effective Explanations & Analogies
- **Transposition as "capo thinking"** -- resonates immediately; same shapes, just shifted
- **Roman numerals as "universal language"** -- helps students see why numbers > letter names
- **Power chords as transposition-friendly** because they lack the 3rd (no major/minor quality)
- **"The distances never change, only the starting position"** -- core transposition insight
- **bVII-to-i as "rock's version of coming home"** vs classical V-i -- makes the stylistic difference click
- **"Scale as alphabet, harmonizing as spelling words"** -- makes the constraint (only diatonic notes) intuitive
- **Thirds as "sweet spot" between crunchy seconds and hollow fifths** -- explains why we stack thirds
- **Counting frets between diad notes** to show why quality is min vs Maj -- concrete, verifiable on guitar
- **"Same seven chords, different home base"** -- relative major/minor concept

## Common Student Misconceptions
- Flat symbols in Roman numerals (bIII, bVI, bVII) are NOT accidentals to add; they describe position relative to major scale
- Open string riffs don't transpose by simple shifting -- need fretted equivalents
- The ii chord in natural minor is diminished, but method books often simplify to plain minor for power-chord contexts
- Students forget to shift ALL notes when transposing (move root but leave passing tones)
- "Flatted" degrees in minor confused with accidentals -- they mean "lowered vs the parallel major," not "add a flat sign"
- Diminished ii chord causes confusion -- important to explain why books alter it to minor (practical, not theoretical)
- Diads confused as "incomplete chords" -- reframe as useful musical tools, not just theory building blocks

## Key Minor Scale Teaching Points
- Natural minor chord quality pattern: min, dim, Maj, min, min, Maj, Maj
- i-bVI-bIII-bVII is the foundational rock/metal minor progression
- Root motion in this progression involves fourths/fifths = satisfying voice leading
- Song examples that work: "Zombie" (Cranberries), "Paranoid" (Sabbath), "All Along the Watchtower"
